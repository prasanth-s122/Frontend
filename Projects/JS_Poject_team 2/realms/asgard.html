Asgard

The Realm of the Aesir

Asgard is the magnificent, heavily fortified home of the Aesir gods, ruled with an iron fist by the All-Father, Odin. Bathed in eternal sunlight, its golden spires and grand halls reflect the immense power, wealth, and unyielding pride of its inhabitants. It is a realm obsessed with military might and prophecy, designed to withstand the inevitable coming of Ragnarok.


The Great Wall of Hrimthur

Surrounding the entirety of the realm is an impenetrable, towering wall, built long ago by a mysterious giant named Hrimthur. The wall protects the sprawling divine city and the plains of Ida from any invasion. Inside the walls lie vast training grounds where the Einherjar—warriors who died bravely in battle—train relentlessly. They prepare endlessly for the end of the world, fighting, dying, and resurrecting daily.


The Grand Lodge and Valhalla

Odin rules from the massive Great Lodge, surveying the Nine Realms through his ravens, Huginn and Muninn. A large portion of the realm is dedicated to the Great Hall of Valhalla, with its roof thatched in golden shields and spears. The environment of Asgard is artificially pristine, hiding the deep paranoia and political machinations of the All-Father and his family.
