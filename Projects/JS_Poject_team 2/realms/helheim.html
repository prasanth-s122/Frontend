Helheim

The Realm of the Dead

Helheim is the frozen, desolate underworld where souls who died of sickness, old age, or cowardice are sentenced to eternity. Unlike Valhalla, which rewards those who died in battle, Helheim is a place of endless sorrow and crushing isolation. It is impossibly cold, reaching temperatures so absolute that no earthly or magical fire can burn there, except the chaotic Blades of Chaos themselves.


The Bridge of the Damned

Souls wander endlessly across the colossal, green, spectral Bridge of the Damned. This bridge is the only entry point into the realm and is vigilantly guarded by the monstrous guardian eagle Hraesvelgr. The realm constantly tortures its inhabitants with agonizing, lifelike illusions of their greatest regrets, forcing them to relive their worst moments until they lose their minds.


The Hel-Walkers

Due to the corruption of the Valkyries by Odin, the gatekeeping mechanism of Helheim was broken, causing dead souls to spill back over into Midgard. These reanimated corpses, known as Hel-Walkers, act purely on instinctual malice and spread the freezing influence of Hel wherever they roam. Helheim is a grim reminder of an afterlife devoid of hope or rest.
