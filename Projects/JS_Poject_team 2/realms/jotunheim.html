Jotunheim

The Realm of the Giants

Jotunheim is the hidden, mountainous homeland of the Giants (Jötnar). It is a sprawling landscape of towering peaks, immense frozen lakes, and colossal statues carved directly into the mountain faces. After the giants were slaughtered by Thor in Midgard, they retreated to Jotunheim and sealed the realm away to hide from Odin's wrath.


The Highest Peak in all the Realms

Jotunheim possesses the highest peak in existence, a singular mountain that towers above the clouds. The Giants were a peaceful, deeply prophetic race with a profound connection to magic and fate. Scattered throughout Jotunheim are the remains of their once-great civilization, holding murals that secretly detail the secrets of the past and the inevitable future.


The Ironwood Forest

A secret, vibrant part of Jotunheim is the hidden sanctuary of Ironwood, ruled by the mysterious giantess Angrboda. Unlike the snowy, barren peaks, Ironwood is painted with deep autumn colors, massive ancient roots, and vibrant wildlife. It serves as the hidden sanctuary where the giants paint the prophecies of the world onto magical shrines, preserving their legacy against the Aesir.
