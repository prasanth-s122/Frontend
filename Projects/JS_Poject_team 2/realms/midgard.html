Midgard

The Realm of Mortals

Midgard is the mortal realm and essentially the focal point of the Nine Realms. Placed precisely in the branches of the World Tree, Yggdrasil, it has long been the battleground where gods, mortals, and monsters intersect. Once a diverse and lush landscape, Midgard has now been plunged into a deep, bitter winter known as Fimbulwinter, a prophetic freeze signaling the impending onset of Ragnarok, the twilight of the gods.


The Lake of Nine & Tyr's Temple

In the very center of Midgard lies the massive Lake of Nine. Dominating the lake's center is Tyr's Temple, a gold-adorned marvel of Norse architecture that serves as the bridge between all realms. Surrounding the temple—and the entire realm—is the World Serpent, Jörmungandr. He is a giant beast of such unimaginable proportions that he is capable of wrapping his body around Midgard completely and biting his own tail. The realm is scattered with the remnants of long-lost mortal civilizations, raider camps driven mad by the freezing wastes, and ancient magical ruins hiding legendary armaments.


The Curse of Fimbulwinter

Before Fimbulwinter, Midgard was a bustling hub of life. Now, the freezing temperatures have frozen the great Lake of Nine solid, allowing travelers to walk atop its icy surface, albeit at their own peril. Wolves, Hel-walkers, and desperate mortals roam the wastes. Midgard represents the resilience of life in the face of inevitable destruction, a dying realm waiting for the prophesied war to wash it away.
